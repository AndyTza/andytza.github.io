My name is Anastasios Tzanidakis, but you can call me Andy. I am a Post-Baccalaureate researcher at California Institute of Technology working with the Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF) survey. I currently work under Professor Mansi Kasliwal on the Census of the Local Universe (CLU) experiment, the largest volumetric-survey, attempting to classify all Supernovae within 400 Mpc.



I recently earned my Bachelors degree in Astronomy from Columbia University in New York City. At Columbia, my primary research was conducted in the field of Galactic Archeology -- mapping the shape of the Milky Way Galaxy. I also had the amazing opportunity to intern at NASA Ames Research Center with the Kepler Guest Observer Office. At NASA I fell in love with open-source software development and its applications to Astrophysical discovery. Overall I love to write software that is applicable to observational astronomy such as data reduction systems, friendly data processing API's that make observational routines more efficient.


Science communication is at the core of my values. I enjoy opportunities to teach the public about the wonders of the universe through podcasting, public speaking events and even hosting workshops for undergraduate students.
