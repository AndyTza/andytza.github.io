Greetings!

My name is Anastasios, but you can call me Andy. I am a post-baccalaureate researcher at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) in affiliation with the Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF) survey. I currently work under professor Mansi Kasliwal on the Census of the Local Universe experiment; aimed to understand the synthesis and classification of supernovae populations within the local universe.

I recently earned my Bachelor's degree in astronomy from Columbia University in New York City. At Columbia, my primary research was conducted in the field of Galactic Archeology, mapping the shape of the Milky Way's disk using astrometric surveys. I also had the amazing opportunity to intern at NASA Ames Research Center with the Kepler Guest Observer Office. At NASA I was a contributor to the python package [lightkurve](https://docs.lightkurve.org/about/citing.html) writing tools for astronomical data processing techniques.

 I also fell in love with open-source software development and its applications to science.

My current academic interest span from galactic evolution to the synthesis of supernovae populations in the era of all-sky surveys. I also love to write software that is applicable to observational astronomy such as data reduction systems, friendly data processing API's that make observational routines more efficient.


Science communication is at the core of my values. I enjoy opportunities to teach the public about the wonders of the universe through podcasting, public speaking events and even hosting workshops for undergraduate students.
